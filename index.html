Hi there! I am Chandra Shekhar Murmu, working as an ARCO prize post-doctoral fellow at the Astrophysics Research Center of the Open University (ARCO) at the Open University of Israel. I am passionate about conducting research in Cosmology and I focus primarily on probing the early Universe with Intensity Mapping, using bright line emission tracers from the galaxies such as the [C II]158μm, CO, [O III]88μm,Ly-\(\alpha\), etc. I also study the [H I]21cm line emission from neutral hydrogen gas originating from the early IGM. These techniques provides a relevant probe of the Epoch of Reionization, the era when the first luminous sources were formed and started illuminating our Universe!

To know more about my work, head to the Research section.

My publications can be found at the Publications section. The tools that I have developed can be found at the Codes section.
